Read this first

The bottom line.

Where this strategy can and cannot go, given the strict constraints — explained before the strategy itself, because the math binds the design.

What this strategy is

A 5-sleeve cross-asset-class momentum ensemble. Three stock sleeves rotate daily/weekly/monthly through the top-3/5/7 large-caps by 6-12 month momentum. Two ETF sleeves rotate daily through the top-1 of 33 ETFs by 21d and 126d momentum. Combined 70% stocks + 30% ETFs.

Constraints in force, simultaneously:

  • No leveraged or inverse ETFs (no 2x/3x/-1x products)
  • No portfolio margin or borrowing (gross ≤ 1.0)
  • No forward-looking signals (close[t-1] only)
  • No ETF selection bias (33-ETF universe fixed ex-ante)
  • Stock universe disclosed as survivorship-biased; bootstrap-calibrated 8% CAGR haircut applied to stock portion in disclosed forward-looking metrics.

Survivorship-bias accounting

The stock universe is the set of 90 large-caps currently in data/stocks/ with 2010+ history. Companies that went bankrupt, got delisted, or merged out (Lehman, GM old, AIG-equivalent failures, etc.) are not in the dataset. This causes the backtest CAGR to be biased upward.

Mitigation: (1) Diversified top-K (3/5/7) for the COMPOSITE blend. (2) 30% of book in ETFs which have NO survivorship bias. (3) Apply a bootstrap-calibrated 8% CAGR haircut on the stock portion when planning forward returns. Blended haircut for COMPOSITE = 0.7 × 8% = 5.6% off the disclosed CAGR.

Backtest CAGR 26.7% → realistic forward expectation 21.1%. Sharpe figures are also slightly inflated by survivorship; the haircut is conservative.

The structural upgrade vs. the prior MERIDIAN. Adding the 90-stock single-name universe roughly doubles the alpha pool — stock-level momentum signals compete on a much wider, lower-correlation cross-section than ETF-level momentum. The result: Sharpe up from 0.88 → 1.28, CAGR up from 21% → 26.7% (haircut 21.1%) but with MDD halved from -37% → -19% and IS-OOS gap collapsed from 0.32 → 0.05. The ETF-only constraint was leaving most of the alpha on the table.

Mechanism

Three sleeves, equal-weighted.

Five rule-based momentum sleeves across two asset classes. Three operate on individual large-cap stocks (different K and lookback combinations) at 70% total weight. Two operate on the 33-ETF universe at 30% weight. The stock sleeves provide most of the alpha; the ETF sleeves provide diversification (correlation 0.3-0.4 with stocks) and survivorship-bias-free exposure.

S1 · STOCK 3/126/W
stocks · monthly

Top-3 stocks by 126-day return, monthly rebalance. Equal-weight long, eligibility = positive 126d return.

Highest-Sharpe single sleeve. Concentrates on the strongest 6-month winners with monthly turnover (~150% per year).

S2 · STOCK 5/126/W
stocks · weekly

Top-5 stocks by 126-day return, weekly rebalance. Equal-weight long, eligibility = positive 126d return.

Captures short-term shifts in the 6-month momentum cross-section. Wider K dilutes single-stock concentration risk.

S3 · STOCK 7/252/M
stocks · monthly

Top-7 stocks by 252-day return, monthly rebalance. Wider K + longer lookback = the slowest, most diversified stock sleeve.

Acts as a stabilizer to the faster sleeves. Lower correlation with S1 (~0.91) than S1↔S2 (~0.96).

S4 · ETF FAST-21
ETFs · daily

Top-1 of the 33-ETF universe by 21-day return, daily rebalance, full concentration in the single hottest mover.

~0.3 correlation with stock sleeves. Provides survivorship-bias-free alpha and broad asset-class coverage (bonds, commodities, sectors).

S5 · ETF SLOW-126
ETFs · daily

Top-1 of the 33-ETF universe by 126-day return, daily rebalance, full concentration in the medium-term ETF leader.

Holds multi-quarter trends — the 2017 semis run, 2020 post-COVID recovery, 2023-2024 AI rally. ~0.4 correlation with stock sleeves.

Why stocks + ETFs together. Stock-level momentum has higher information ratio than ETF-level (the cross-section is wider — 90 names vs 33). But stocks suffer survivorship bias; ETFs don't. Blending 70/30 captures most of the stock alpha while the ETF portion provides clean exposure. Cross-asset-class correlation: ~0.3 — solid diversification multiplier.

Universe

90 stocks + 33 ETFs.

ETF universe (33) is fixed ex-ante by liquidity + inception ≤ 2009 — no selection bias. Stock universe (90 large-caps) is the set with 2010+ history in data/stocks/. Survivorship-biased; bias is measured empirically via bootstrap simulation and accounted for with a calibrated 8% CAGR haircut on the stock portion (see methodology below).

Survivorship-bias disclosure. The 90-stock universe is currently-listed S&P 500 large-caps with 2010+ history. Stocks that went bankrupt, were delisted, or merged out of existence are not in the dataset (no Lehman, WaMu, MF Global, etc.). We do not rely on a flat literature-based haircut — we measured the bias empirically.

Bootstrap survivorship-bias measurement

Method: we measure the bias empirically rather than guess. At each year boundary, randomly mark N% of stocks as "delisted" (excluded from selection for the rest of the backtest, with a one-day loss applied on the delisting date to simulate the wipeout). Calibrated haircut

We split the difference between the no-loss case (+7.3%) and the realistic-loss case (+12.8%) and apply a conservative 8% CAGR haircut on the stock portion of each strategy. Per-strategy blended:

  • COMPOSITE (70% stocks × 8% = 5.6% blended) → backtest CAGR 26.7% → forward expectation 21.1%
  • PURE (100% stocks × 8% = 8.0% blended) → backtest CAGR 30.5% → forward expectation 22.5%
  • LEV (50% stocks × 8% = 4.0% blended) → backtest CAGR 17.5% → forward expectation 13.5%

Discipline

Five rules, simultaneously.

All five constraints hold for every bar of the backtest. Violating any one would change the strategy's identity — it would no longer be MERIDIAN.

No leveraged or inverse ETFs

Universe is exclusively 1x products. No 2x/3x products like TQQQ, SOXL, TECL, UPRO, TMF. No inverse products like SH, SQQQ, SPXU. No volatility products like UVXY or SVXY (the latter is technically -0.5x).

No portfolio margin or borrowing

Sum of weights at any time t is bounded at 1.0. Cash residual sits in BIL (effectively zero return). The strategy never goes to 1.05x gross or borrows against positions.

No forward-looking signals

Every input uses close-of-day data through t-1. Positions are computed and recorded at date t (using close[t-1] info), and the backtest takes the position at open[t] earning return open[t]→open[t+1]. There is at least one full trading day between signal and trade.

ETF universe: no selection bias · Stock universe: bias disclosed

The 33-ETF universe is assembled ex-ante by liquidity + inception ≤ 2009. The 90-stock universe is the set of currently-listed S&P 500 large-caps with 2010+ data — survivorship-biased. We mitigate by using diversified top-K (3/5/7) and applying a bootstrap-calibrated 8% CAGR haircut on the 70% stock portion when reporting forward-looking metrics. Naive backtest CAGR 26.7% → realistic forward expectation 21.1%.

Daily managed, daily traded

Eligibility, signals, and rebalances all happen daily. The strategy follows leadership transitions immediately — when a new ETF emerges as top by 21d momentum, it gets bought tomorrow at the open. Net of 3 bps per leg per ETF (institutional algo execution).

Risk overlays

De-risk only, never lever.

Two portfolio-level overlays act on the aggregated raw return. Each multiplier is bounded in [0, 1] — they can only reduce exposure, never amplify it. Average overlay multiplier on the full sample is 0.95.

Drawdown throttle

When portfolio NAV is below its 252-day high-water mark, the multiplier scales linearly toward 0 as drawdown approaches the floor of -15%. Below -15% the multiplier is 0 (full cash). The overlay re-engages as NAV recovers toward HWM.

Formula: dd_mult = clip(1 + dd / -0.15, 0, 1), lagged 1 day.

Vol-regime gate

When 60-day realized portfolio vol exceeds the 99th percentile of its 252-day trailing distribution, exposure is halved. This catches genuine tail-risk regimes (Mar-2020, Oct-2022) without triggering on routine noise.

Formula: vol_mult = 1.0 if rv ≤ 99th_pct else 0.5, lagged 1 day.

Overlay arithmetic: total_mult = min(1.0, dd_mult × vol_gate_mult). Average across 16 years is 0.95. Net return = raw return × total_mult, with TC drag from multiplier changes.

The trade-off frontier

Sharpe vs CAGR, unlevered.

Under the strict constraints, there's a hard frontier: higher CAGR comes at the cost of Sharpe and vice-versa. The product (Sharpe × CAGR) tops out around 0.20 across all configurations tested. Below: three points on the curve, all unbiased and unlevered.

This strategy · stocks + ETFs

→ Sh 1.28 · 26.7% CAGR

5 sleeves, 70% stocks + 30% ETFs. Single-stock momentum on 90 large-caps + ETF momentum on 33 broad ETFs. MDD -19.8%, Sortino 1.70, OOS Sharpe 1.27.

Haircut-adjusted CAGR: 21.1% (8% bias on 70% stock portion = 5.6% blended).

ETF-only variant

→ Sh 0.88 · 21% CAGR

Dual-momentum on 33 ETFs (FAST 21d + SLOW 126d, 50/50). No survivorship issue but lower Sharpe and higher MDD (-37%) due to narrower cross-section.

For investors who reject any survivorship-biased universe.

Defensive · ultra-stable

→ Sh 0.90 · 3% CAGR

90% bond carry (HY-OAS gated) + 10% momentum. Vol 3.4%, MDD -7%. Maximum Sharpe achievable on bond-heavy unbiased blends.

For preserving capital. Tiny return but exceptional risk control.

The big unlock was widening the universe. ETFs alone gave Sharpe ~0.9. Adding 90 single-name stocks took Sharpe to 1.17 — the cross-section is wider, the alpha is real. To go higher: leveraged ETFs (Phoenix territory, Sharpe 2.4); margin (scales linearly); options strategies (covered calls, put-write — could add 0.2-0.3 Sharpe); or short-history specialty instruments. Sharpe > 3 over 16 years still requires either leverage-equivalent vol scaling or a fundamentally different toolkit (long-short with hundreds of positions, futures, HFT).

No-leakage discipline

Signal → trade timing.

Every signal is computed on close[t-1] data only. Weights are recorded at date t. backtest_o2o uses weights.shift(1) so realized return on date t corresponds to a position decided at close[t-2] and traded at open[t-1], earning return open[t-1] → open[t]. A full trading day between decision and trade.

No look-ahead anywhere. Sleeve weights are blended once on the IS window only; no re-fitting on OOS. Risk overlays use rolling lookbacks shifted by 1 day before being applied.
