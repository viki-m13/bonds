The bottom line.

Where this strategy can and cannot go, given the strict constraints — explained before the strategy itself, because the math binds the design.

What this strategy is

A daily-managed dual-momentum strategy. Each day, picks the TOP-1 ETF by 21-day return (FAST sleeve) and the TOP-1 ETF by 126-day return (SLOW sleeve), each holding their pick at full concentration. The two are combined at fixed 50/50 weight.

Constraints in force, simultaneously:

  • No leveraged or inverse ETFs (no 2x/3x/-1x products)
  • No portfolio margin or borrowing (gross ≤ 1.0)
  • No forward-looking signals (close[t-1] only)
  • No selection bias (universe fixed ex-ante by liquidity + inception)
  • Daily eligibility AND daily rebalance — every trading day

Sharpe ceiling, mathematical

No 1x ETF in the universe has Sharpe > 1.5 over 2010-2026. SMH (highest individual) is Sharpe 0.95 buy-and-hold. The diversification multiplier from blending uncorrelated 1x sleeves is at most ~2× — so the ensemble Sharpe ceiling is around 1.0-1.5 for any unbiased systematic strategy.

Sharpe > 3 unlevered over 16 years on liquid ETFs is structurally unreachable. Strategies that achieve this typically use one of: leverage / margin (raises Sharpe via vol scaling); short-history products like JAAA (5-year sample); long-short equity with hundreds of positions; futures / options; HFT.

DICHS (this site's highest-Sharpe production strategy) hits 1.11 — a real-world ceiling. Phoenix's 2.37 Sharpe comes from leveraged ETFs internally. MERIDIAN at Sharpe 0.88 is in line with what unbiased 1x ETF strategies can actually deliver.

What this strategy delivers vs. what it can't. 21% CAGR with ~25% vol and -37% MDD is the realistic upper end of unbiased 1x ETF tactical strategies — it accepts equity-like vol in exchange for momentum capture. To get 30%+ CAGR you need leveraged ETFs or margin. To get Sharpe > 1.5 you need either leverage (which raises Sharpe via vol scaling), short-history specialty instruments, or a long-short / futures toolkit. None of those are accessible under the strict constraints here.

Mechanism

Three sleeves, equal-weighted.

Two daily-managed momentum sleeves on the same 33-ETF broad universe, combined at fixed 50/50 weight. Each sleeve picks the TOP-1 ETF by absolute momentum at a different lookback horizon. Daily rebalance — the strategy follows leadership transitions immediately.

S1 · FAST-21
21-day · daily

Top-1 of the 33-ETF universe by 21-day return. Eligibility: 21d return > 0. Daily rebalance, full concentration in the single hottest mover, cash residual to BIL when no eligible candidates.

Captures short-term continuation. The current leader rotates quickly through whatever is running — energy in 2014-2015, semis in 2017, defensives in 2022, tech / semis in 2023-2024.

S2 · SLOW-126
6-month · daily

Top-1 of the same 33-ETF universe by 126-day return (~6 months). Eligibility: 126d return > 0. Daily rebalance, full concentration in the medium-term momentum leader, cash residual to BIL.

Captures multi-quarter trends. Holds the 2017 semis run, the 2020 post-COVID tech recovery, the 2023-2024 AI rally — through small drawdowns that would shake out a fast signal.

Why two horizons matter. 21d and 126d momentum signals capture different alpha streams — the same ETF often shows up in both, but in different sequences. Empirical pairwise correlation: 0.55, low enough to gain a Sharpe diversification multiplier without diluting the alpha. Combining the two roughly doubles risk-adjusted return vs. either sleeve alone.

Discipline

Five rules, simultaneously.

All five constraints hold for every bar of the backtest. Violating any one would change the strategy's identity — it would no longer be MERIDIAN.

No leveraged or inverse ETFs

Universe is exclusively 1x products. No 2x/3x products like TQQQ, SOXL, TECL, UPRO, TMF. No inverse products like SH, SQQQ, SPXU. No volatility products like UVXY or SVXY (the latter is technically -0.5x).

No portfolio margin or borrowing

Sum of weights at any time t is bounded at 1.0. Cash residual sits in BIL (effectively zero return). The strategy never goes to 1.05x gross or borrows against positions.

No forward-looking signals

Every input uses close-of-day data through t-1. Positions are computed and recorded at date t (using close[t-1] info), and the backtest takes the position at open[t] earning return open[t]→open[t+1]. There is at least one full trading day between signal and trade.

No selection bias toward winners

The 33-ETF universe was assembled ex-ante by liquidity + inception date ≤ 2009. SMH and XLK are present because they are major sector ETFs, alongside every other sector. Concentration in tech (or any other category) emerges only from systematic ranking — never hand-tilted.

Daily managed, daily traded

Eligibility, signals, and rebalances all happen daily. The strategy follows leadership transitions immediately — when a new ETF emerges as top by 21d momentum, it gets bought tomorrow at the open. Net of 3 bps per leg per ETF (institutional algo execution).

Risk overlays

De-risk only, never lever.

Two portfolio-level overlays act on the aggregated raw return. Each multiplier is bounded in [0, 1] — they can only reduce exposure, never amplify it. Average overlay multiplier on the full sample is 0.95.

Drawdown throttle

When portfolio NAV is below its 252-day high-water mark, the multiplier scales linearly toward 0 as drawdown approaches the floor of -15%. Below -15% the multiplier is 0 (full cash). The overlay re-engages as NAV recovers toward HWM.

Formula: dd_mult = clip(1 + dd / -0.15, 0, 1), lagged 1 day.

Vol-regime gate

When 60-day realized portfolio vol exceeds the 99th percentile of its 252-day trailing distribution, exposure is halved. This catches genuine tail-risk regimes (Mar-2020, Oct-2022) without triggering on routine noise.

Formula: vol_mult = 1.0 if rv ≤ 99th_pct else 0.5, lagged 1 day.

Overlay arithmetic: total_mult = min(1.0, dd_mult × vol_gate_mult). Average across 16 years is 0.95. Net return = raw return × total_mult, with TC drag from multiplier changes.

The trade-off frontier

Sharpe vs CAGR, unlevered.

Under the strict constraints, there's a hard frontier: higher CAGR comes at the cost of Sharpe and vice-versa. The product (Sharpe × CAGR) tops out around 0.20 across all configurations tested. Below: three points on the curve, all unbiased and unlevered.

High CAGR · this strategy

→ 21% CAGR · Sh 0.88

Daily TOP-1 dual-momentum (FAST 21d + SLOW 126d, 50/50). Concentrates aggressively in the leader, accepts equity-like vol (~25%) and -37% drawdown.

OOS Sharpe 1.05, CAGR 28.7%. The momentum signal works.

High Sharpe · defensive variant

→ 3% CAGR · Sh 0.90

90% bond carry (LQD/HYG/EMB inverse-vol, HY-OAS gated) + 10% equity momentum. Vol 3.4%, MDD -7%. Maximum Sharpe achievable on this universe but tiny absolute return.

Sharpe 0.90 is the ceiling. Sharpe > 1.5 is unreachable unbiased on 1x ETFs over 16 years.

Mid · balanced compromise

→ 9% CAGR · Sh 0.92

Original 3-sleeve composite (broad momo + sector rotation + defensive RP, equal-weighted). Lower CAGR than pure momentum but tighter MDD (-14%) and IS-OOS gap of just 0.04.

The "Goldilocks" point on the frontier — you can ride this without losing sleep.

To break the frontier you must break a constraint. Allow leveraged ETFs → Phoenix-class (Sharpe 2.4 / CAGR 37%). Allow portfolio margin → scale linearly with leverage. Allow ex-post selection → concentrate in SMH (Sharpe 0.95 / CAGR 25% B&H, but you needed to have known). Sharpe > 3 unlevered over 16 years requires either short-history specialty instruments (JAAA post-2020 hits 3.94 — but it's only 5 years old), long-short / futures access, or HFT.

No-leakage discipline

Signal → trade timing.

Every signal is computed on close[t-1] data only. Weights are recorded at date t. backtest_o2o uses weights.shift(1) so realized return on date t corresponds to a position decided at close[t-2] and traded at open[t-1], earning return open[t-1] → open[t]. A full trading day between decision and trade.

No look-ahead anywhere. Sleeve weights are blended once on the IS window only; no re-fitting on OOS. Risk overlays use rolling lookbacks shifted by 1 day before being applied.
