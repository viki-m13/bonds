Strategy Overview

This is a blended strategy combining two uncorrelated alpha sources with ZERO daily scaling.

Objective: Generate consistent, positive risk-adjusted returns with low correlation to traditional stock and bond indices. The strategy harvests carry (income) premia across multiple asset classes while hedging unwanted market exposures using inverse ETFs.

How It Works: Each month, the strategy evaluates 95 candidate "carry streams" — each a long position in a yield-bearing ETF paired with an inverse ETF hedge. The top 5 streams ranked by trailing 1-year Sharpe ratio are selected and weighted proportionally. All positions are long-only ETF purchases — no shorting, no margin, no derivatives.

Asset Classes: Investment-grade bonds, high-yield bonds, emerging market debt, CLOs, MLPs, dividend equities, REITs, commodities, crypto (BTC/ETH/SOL via ETFs), currencies, and managed futures. Inverse treasury and equity ETFs (TBF, TYO, SH) are used as hedges.

Pricing & Execution: Backtest vs. Real Life
In the Backtest
The signal is computed using closing prices through day T-1. New portfolio weights are applied starting day T. The return earned on day T = Close(T) ÷ Close(T-1) − 1.

This effectively assumes you entered the new positions at T-1's closing price — the last price before the signal was generated.
In Real Life
You see the updated allocations on the evening of T-1 (after market close). You place your buy/sell orders. Your orders fill at day T's opening price.

Your actual entry price is T's open, not T-1's close. The difference is the overnight gap — typically 1-5 basis points for bond and dividend ETFs.

How the Model Works — Step by Step

Every 21 trading days, the model runs this exact process using only data available at that point. No future information is ever used.

Step 1: Generate 112 Candidate Streams

Each "stream" is a daily return series built from one of 10 engines. The two main types:

Carry Streams (no signal — runs every day)
A fixed-weight blend of a yield-bearing ETF + an inverse ETF hedge. Example: carry_hyg_tbf = 67% HYG + 33% TBF every day. The carry (income) from HYG is captured while TBF hedges out interest rate risk. About 70 streams are carry-based.

Momentum Streams (signal-driven)
A position that varies between 0% and 100% based on trailing momentum. The signal averages 21/63/126/252-day risk-adjusted returns. If the signal is positive, go long; if negative, stay in cash. About 40 streams are momentum-based.

Step 2: Vol-Target Each Stream to 3%

Each stream's daily return is scaled so its rolling 63-day volatility equals 3% annualized. This makes all streams comparable — a low-vol CLO stream gets scaled UP, a high-vol crypto stream gets scaled DOWN. The scaler always uses yesterday's volatility (no look-ahead).

Step 3: Compute Trailing 252-Day Sharpe for Each Stream

Using the last year of vol-targeted returns: Sharpe = mean / std × √252. Only streams with trailing Sharpe > 0.75 pass the quality filter. This ensures only streams with strong recent risk-adjusted performance are considered.

Step 4: Select Top 5 by Trailing Sharpe

Sort all qualifying streams by their trailing Sharpe ratio. Take the top 5. The number of qualifying streams varies — as of the latest rebalance, 33 of 112 streams had Sharpe > 0.75.

Step 5: Weight by Sharpe-Squared

Each position's weight is proportional to its trailing Sharpe squared. This concentrates capital into the highest-conviction stream. If stream A has Sharpe 2.0 and stream B has Sharpe 1.0, A gets 4× the weight of B (not 2×). Weights are capped at the 90th percentile to prevent extreme concentration.

Step 6: Hold for 21 Trading Days

The portfolio earns the weighted sum of its 5 streams' daily returns. No changes until the next rebalance. The carry streams run on autopilot (fixed weights); the momentum streams adjust their position daily based on their own signal, but which streams are selected only changes at rebalance.

Step 7: Risk Controls (Applied Daily)

Three adjustments are applied to the combined portfolio return each day, all using prior-day data only:

VIX Scaling: If VIX is in its 90th percentile (high fear), reduce exposure to ~50%. If low VIX, allow up to 120%. Formula: scale = 1.2 − 0.8 × VIX_percentile, clipped to [0.5, 1.2].

Drawdown Control: If the portfolio is in a drawdown, reduce exposure exponentially. At −10% drawdown, exposure drops to ~60%. At −20%, it drops to ~37%. Formula: scale = e^(drawdown × 5), clipped to [0.2, 1.0].

Vol Targeting: Scale the entire portfolio so its rolling 63-day volatility equals 10% annualized. If recent vol was 15%, the scaler is 10/15 = 0.67. If vol was 5%, scaler is 10/5 = 2.0.

What Runs Daily vs Monthly — Important

The backtest applies several adjustments DAILY, not just at monthly rebalance. This materially affects performance.

Recommendation: At minimum, implement daily per-stream volatility targeting. This is the biggest driver of backtest performance. Without it, expected Sharpe drops from 1.67 to 1.16. The VIX/drawdown scaling adds minimal value (~0.09 Sharpe) and can be skipped. In practice, daily vol targeting means checking each stream's recent volatility and adjusting your dollar exposure so each stream contributes ~3% annualized vol to the portfolio.

The strategy harvests carry premia — the yield income from bonds, dividends, and CLOs — while hedging out the main risk factors (interest rates, equity market) using inverse ETFs. The adaptive selection automatically rotates into whichever carry streams are performing best, and the risk controls cut exposure during crises. The combination of diversified carry + dynamic selection + risk management produces a consistently positive return with controlled drawdowns.
