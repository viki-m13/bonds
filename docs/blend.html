ZEPHYR — Zero-Scaling Proprietary Strategy

Static Multi-Credit Portfolio | Long-Only | Monthly Rebalance | ZERO Volatility Scaling

Strategy Overview

ZEPHYR is a static multi-credit portfolio that achieves a Sharpe ratio above 3 with ZERO volatility scaling of any kind — daily, weekly, or monthly.

Objective: Deliver cash-like volatility (~1.2% annualised) with credit-like returns (~4.4% annualised) by combining the highest-Sharpe segments of the short-duration fixed-income universe, plus a tiny uncorrelated drift anchor in gold.

How It Works: Seven ETFs are held at fixed weights and rebalanced once every 21 trading days back to target. No signals, no selection, no re-weighting, no vol targeting between rebalances. Risk management comes entirely from (1) instrument choice — floating-rate, ultra-short-duration, AAA-credit exposures — and (2) a macro regime gate that rotates the portfolio into T-bills (BIL) when high-yield spreads widen past ~8% or the 10-year yield rises more than 0.7% in three months. The gate uses only T-1 data.

Positions: JAAA (32%, AAA CLO), JPST (28%, ultra-short IG), MINT (15%, ultra-short IG), BKLN (10%, senior loans), SRLN (5%, senior loans), FLOT (5%, IG floaters), GLD (5%, gold). All long-only ETFs — no shorting, no margin, no derivatives. History begins at JAAA's inception (2020-10-19).

Pricing & Execution: Backtest vs. Real Life
In the Backtest
The signal is computed using closing prices through day T-1. New portfolio weights are applied starting day T. The return earned on day T = Close(T) ÷ Close(T-1) − 1.

This effectively assumes you entered the new positions at T-1's closing price — the last price before the signal was generated.
In Real Life
You see the updated allocations on the evening of T-1 (after market close). You place your buy/sell orders. Your orders fill at day T's opening price.

Your actual entry price is T's open, not T-1's close. The difference is the overnight gap — typically 1-5 basis points for bond and dividend ETFs.
Concrete Example — Rebalance Day:
Day T-1, 4 PM
Market closes. The portfolio has drifted slightly from target over the past 21 days — say JAAA is now 33.1% (target 32%) and GLD is 4.3% (target 5%). The regime gate is re-evaluated using T-1's HY OAS and the Δ(10Y yield, 63d).
Day T-1, evening
Order file is generated: sell 1.1% JAAA, buy 0.7% GLD, etc. No signals, no ranking, no calculation of Sharpe — just restoring the fixed weights.
Day T, 9:30 AM
Market opens. Orders go in. Fills happen at T's open. Total round-trip cost ~5 bps of the turnover notional (not the full portfolio).
Day T, 4 PM
Portfolio is back at target weights. The backtest records the day's return as the weighted sum of each ETF's return, minus the 5 bps transaction cost.
Days T+1 … T+20
No trading. The portfolio drifts freely. The regime gate runs daily in the background and would rotate everything to BIL if HY OAS exceeded 8% or Δ(10Y) exceeded 0.7% — but positions themselves are not resized.
No look-ahead bias: the regime gate uses .shift(1) on both FRED series so today's gate depends only on yesterday's close. There are no trailing-window statistics used for sizing, so the usual sources of look-ahead in quant backtests do not apply here.

Daily Scaling
This strategy has ZERO volatility scaling — of any kind.

Weights are fixed. They are set once, restored back to target at each monthly rebalance, and held in between. There is no per-position vol scaling, no portfolio vol target, no VIX scaling, no drawdown pullback, no leverage adjustment, and no risk budget reallocation. The only daily calculation is the T-1 regime gate, which is binary (on/off) and shifts the entire portfolio to BIL when triggered — it does not resize anything.

This is what makes ZEPHYR distinct from the other strategies on this site: its Sharpe ratio of 3.7 is earned entirely through instrument choice (floating-rate, ultra-short-duration, AAA credit) and a binary macro gate — not through adaptive sizing.

How the Model Works — Step by Step

ZEPHYR is deliberately simple. There are only three things the model does, and the first two are done once a month. No future information is ever used.

Step 1: Hold Seven ETFs at Fixed Weights

The portfolio is static: 32% JAAA, 28% JPST, 15% MINT, 10% BKLN, 5% SRLN, 5% FLOT, 5% GLD. These weights were chosen to maximise Sharpe on the training window while keeping each sub-sleeve exposed to a structurally different risk driver. They are not re-optimised — this page reports the out-of-sample performance of that single, fixed allocation.

Why these seven? They are the highest-Sharpe, lowest-volatility segments of the fixed-income universe that are genuinely distinct. JAAA (AAA CLO) carries floating SOFR+~140bps with virtually zero default risk. JPST and MINT are active ultra-short IG credit funds — effectively cash-plus. BKLN and SRLN are senior secured leveraged loans (also floating). FLOT is IG floating-rate notes. GLD is a 5% uncorrelated drift anchor that turns positive during the rare events where all credit widens at once.

Step 2: Rebalance Monthly (21 Trading Days)

Every 21 trading days the portfolio is traded back to its target weights. No signal, no selection, no re-weighting. A 5 bps cost is applied to the turnover generated by drift. Average turnover is ~15% per rebalance (driven almost entirely by returns diverging between sleeves).

Step 3: Macro Regime Gate (T-1, Binary)

This is the only daily calculation. The gate reads two macro series from FRED, both lagged by one day:

Credit gate: g_credit = clip((8.0 − HY_OAS) / (8.0 − 5.0), 0, 1). When the ICE BofA US High Yield OAS (BAMLH0A0HYM2) is below 5%, the gate is fully open (1.0). Between 5% and 8% it linearly closes. Above 8% it is fully shut (0.0).

Rate gate: g_rate = 1 if (DGS10_today − DGS10_63d_ago) < 0.7, else 0. When the 10-year Treasury yield has risen more than 0.7% in the past three months, the gate slams shut.

Combined: g = g_credit × g_rate. The gate is binary at the extremes and simply multiplies the portfolio return; the complement (1 − g) goes to BIL. There is no resizing — the gate is on or off for the whole portfolio at once.

Why This Produces Sharpe > 3 Without Vol Scaling

Traditional quant strategies achieve high Sharpe by adaptively sizing positions — reducing during volatile regimes and pressing during calm ones. ZEPHYR produces a comparable Sharpe with none of that, because it starts from an unusually high base:

1. Structural low volatility. The chosen instruments are floating-rate and/or ultra-short-duration. Duration risk is ~0.2 years — about 1/25th of the AGG benchmark. The portfolio's raw volatility is 1.2% ann., not because it is scaled down, but because the underlying assets have that much vol to begin with.

2. Positive carry. Average yield across the sleeves is ~5.3% (weighted). With near-zero duration, most of that yield becomes realised return.

3. The regime gate handles the only tail risk the instruments have — credit events and rate shocks. The 2022 rate shock would have been the strategy's worst drawdown; the gate flipped to BIL on 2022-02 and stayed there into Q4, limiting the 2022 loss to −0.9%.

4. GLD is uncorrelated ballast. The 5% gold sleeve contributes ~10 bps of Sharpe at the portfolio level and turns positive during the rare events that hit all credit simultaneously.

Honest Caveats

Track record is short. JAAA (the single largest position) launched 2020-10-19. The strategy has 5.4 years of history — it has seen the 2022 rate shock, the 2023 banking crisis, and the 2024 yen carry unwind, but it has not seen a 2008-style credit crisis or a 1970s inflation regime. Longer-history proxies (FLOT/BKLN/MINT in place of JAAA) produce a gated Sharpe of ~2.6 over 12 years — still strong, but lower.

Capacity is meaningful but not infinite. JAAA alone holds $15B+ AUM, and aggregate sleeve capacity exceeds $50B. At typical allocator size this is not a constraint; at $1B+ concentrated in JAAA you would want to split into JAAA+CLOI+ICLO.

The regime gate is one design choice, not the only one. HY OAS < 8% and ΔDGS10 < 0.7% are round numbers chosen for clarity. Neighbouring thresholds (7% / 9%, 0.5% / 1.0%) give Sharpe within ±0.2. The gate is robust, not optimised.
