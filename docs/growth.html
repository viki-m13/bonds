Strategy Overview

Objective: Maximize total portfolio growth over time. Unlike the Sharpe-optimized DICHS strategy, this version prioritizes compounding returns over risk-adjusted performance, accepting higher volatility and drawdowns in exchange for substantially higher long-term wealth accumulation.

How It Works: Each month, the strategy evaluates 66 candidate growth streams — including unhedged momentum, leveraged ETFs (3x), crypto, and high-yield carry. The top 3 streams ranked by trailing 1-year return-squared (not Sharpe) are selected and weighted proportionally. 30% annual vol target — 3x more aggressive than the Sharpe strategy. All positions are long-only ETF purchases.

Asset Classes: Leveraged equity ETFs (TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL), crypto (BTC/ETH/SOL/ADA), high-yield bonds, MLPs, dividend equities, CLOs, and unhedged momentum across equities, commodities, and international markets. Minimal hedging — growth over protection.

Pricing & Execution: Backtest vs. Real Life
In the Backtest
The signal is computed using closing prices through day T-1. New portfolio weights are applied starting day T. The return earned on day T = Close(T) ÷ Close(T-1) − 1.

This effectively assumes you entered the new positions at T-1's closing price — the last price before the signal was generated.
In Real Life
You see the updated allocations on the evening of T-1 (after market close). You place your buy/sell orders. Your orders fill at day T's opening price.

Your actual entry price is T's open, not T-1's close. The difference is the overnight gap — typically 1-5 basis points for bond and dividend ETFs.
Concrete Example — Rebalance on April 27, 2018:
Thu Apr 26, 4 PM
Market closes. Closing prices are recorded. The strategy now has all the data it needs.
Thu Apr 26, evening
Strategy computes trailing 252-day Sharpe for each of 95 streams using closes from Apr 27, 2017 through Apr 26, 2018. Top 5 are selected. Weights are assigned. You now know exactly what to buy and how much.
Fri Apr 27, 9:30 AM
Market opens. You place your trades. Your fill price is the opening price on Apr 27.
Fri Apr 27, 4 PM
Market closes. The backtest records the day's return as Close(Apr 27)/Close(Apr 26) − 1 with the new weights. Your actual return started from the open, not the prior close — the difference is the overnight gap.
Mon Apr 30 onward
Portfolio continues unchanged. No trades until the next rebalance (~21 trading days later, around May 29).
No look-ahead bias: The signal on Apr 26 evening uses zero information from Apr 27 or later. Confirmed by code (eval_data = vol_t.iloc[i-252:i], Python excludes index i) and by autocorrelation test (lag-1 = 0.052, well below 0.1 threshold).

How the Model Works — Step by Step

The Growth strategy follows the same 7-step process as the Sharpe strategy but with parameters tuned for maximum total return instead of maximum risk-adjusted return. The key differences are highlighted below.

Step 1: Generate 66 Candidate Streams

The Growth strategy draws from a different stream pool than Sharpe. Key differences:

Unhedged Carry (higher return, higher risk)
Instead of pairing HYG with inverse TBF, the Growth strategy holds HYG directly — no hedge. This keeps the full yield AND the price appreciation, at the cost of more volatility. Applied to HYG, JNK, BKLN, AMLP, EMB, EMLC, PFF.

Leveraged ETFs (3x amplification)
Includes TQQQ (3x NASDAQ), UPRO (3x S&P), SOXL (3x Semiconductor), TECL (3x Tech), and others. These amplify momentum signals for higher returns. Not used in the Sharpe strategy.

Crypto Momentum (higher allocation)
Same momentum signals as Sharpe (BTC, ETH, SOL, ADA price trends) but crypto streams are given equal opportunity to compete with other streams, not limited by low Sharpe thresholds.

Lightly Hedged (minimal hedge)
Some streams use only 15% inverse allocation (vs 30-50% in Sharpe). Just enough to dampen the worst crashes while preserving most of the upside. Example: 85% QQQ + 15% SQQQ.

Step 2: Vol-Target Each Stream to 5%

Higher than Sharpe's 3%. Each stream runs at 5% annualized volatility, allowing more return potential per stream before portfolio-level scaling.

Step 3: Compute Trailing 252-Day Return for Each Stream

Key difference: The Growth strategy selects by trailing return, not Sharpe ratio. It picks whatever made the most money over the last year, regardless of how volatile the path was. Only streams with positive trailing return are considered.

Step 4: Select Top 3 by Trailing Return

Key difference: Only 3 positions instead of 5. More concentrated = higher returns when right, bigger losses when wrong.

Step 5: Weight by Return-Squared

Key difference: Weights are proportional to trailing return² (not Sharpe²). If stream A returned +30% and stream B returned +15%, A gets 4× the weight. This double-concentrates into the biggest winner.

Step 6: Hold for 21 Trading Days

Same as Sharpe — portfolio runs unchanged until next rebalance.

Step 7: Lighter Risk Controls

VIX Scaling (lighter): Only reduces to 70% at extreme VIX (vs 50% for Sharpe). Formula: scale = 1.1 − 0.3 × VIX_percentile, clipped to [0.7, 1.1].

Drawdown Control: OFF. The Growth strategy does not reduce exposure during drawdowns. It lets positions ride through crashes, which is why max drawdown is larger (~33% vs ~15%) but long-term compounding is higher.

Vol Targeting (higher): Targets 30% annualized volatility (vs 10% for Sharpe). This is the main driver of higher returns — 3× the risk budget means ~3× the return potential.

Why Growth Returns More (and Risks More)

The Growth strategy makes three deliberate tradeoffs: (1) it removes most hedges, keeping the full upside of carry and momentum; (2) it runs at 3× the volatility, amplifying both gains and losses; (3) it doesn't cut exposure in drawdowns, allowing positions to recover instead of locking in losses. The result is ~50% higher annualized return but ~2× deeper drawdowns. This is suited for investors with a long time horizon (10+ years) who can tolerate significant interim losses for higher terminal wealth.
