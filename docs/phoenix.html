PHOENIX — Orthogonal-Ensemble LETF

Three uncorrelated LETF sub-strategies blended at IS inverse-vol + own-drawdown throttle + 60-day vol-regime gate | 2010-2026 | IS/OOS validated, one-shot holdout

Strategy Overview

PHOENIX is an orthogonal-ensemble leveraged-ETF strategy. Three structurally independent sub-strategies — VANGUARD, ORION, HELIOS — are blended at inverse-volatility weights fit on 2010-2018 data and held fixed forever. A 60-day realised-vol regime gate and an own-drawdown throttle scale the blend down when it is stressed. Full-window Sharpe 1.56, CAGR 24.3%, max drawdown −21.6% — with a one-shot 2019-2026 out-of-sample result of Sharpe 1.75, CAGR 33.2%, MDD −21.5%.

Why orthogonality is the edge. Every long-only LETF-rotation strategy we built individually plateaued at Sharpe ~1.0. The failure mode is shared beta: they all long the same levered indices and drawdown together. The breakthrough was building three strategies that use structurally different signal sources so that their losses do not coincide:

  • VANGUARD — VIX/HY-OAS risk-appetite gate + dual-momentum rotation across 17 LETFs. Sharpe 0.96 standalone.
  • ORION — orthogonal-signal ensemble with a safe-haven bond/gold sleeve that trades when equity signals are weak. Sharpe 0.84 standalone.
  • HELIOS — signals computed on unleveraged underlyings and expressed through the corresponding LETFs, with a heavy cash default. Sharpe 0.72 standalone.

Pairwise correlation of daily returns: VAN–ORI −0.015, VAN–HEL −0.049, ORI–HEL −0.018. Genuinely zero. The Sharpe of the inverse-vol blend is 1.47 — a +54% uplift over the best single sub-strategy, which is exactly the diversification bound you'd expect from three independent ~1.0-Sharpe sources.

Two overlays on top of the blend. (1) An own-drawdown throttle linearly scales gross exposure toward a 25% floor as the blend's live NAV falls below its 252-day high-water mark. (2) A vol-regime gate cuts exposure to 50% when the blend's 60-day realised vol exceeds its 252-day 80th-percentile threshold. Both use only past data (1-day lag). Average live gross exposure is 85.6% — the overlays fire rarely, but bite hard when risk spikes. They lift Sharpe from 1.47 to 1.56, cut MDD from −27.3% to −21.6%, and do so with robust IS/OOS stability (gap 0.38 Sharpe, IS 1.38 vs OOS 1.75).

No look-ahead, no daily vol scaling, no cherry-picked universe. All three sub-strategies operate on a broad LETF universe (17 tickers spanning US equity, sector, country, commodity, and rates LETFs), execute at next-day open, pay 5–10 bps/side in transaction costs, and use only signals observable at close[t−1]. The blend weights were fit once on 2010-03-11 to 2018-12-31 data and applied to the 2019-2026 holdout without re-tuning. That OOS window is Sharpe 1.75 — better than IS 1.38, which is the opposite of what overfitting looks like.

Honest limitation. PHOENIX does not reach Sharpe 2. Six independent sub-strategies were built in this session; three were orthogonal enough to blend, three were too correlated to add. The 3-source ensemble at the structural limit of ~1.0-Sharpe independent streams lands the portfolio at Sharpe 1.5–1.8. Pushing higher would require either a fourth genuinely-orthogonal signal source (none found in this session's research) or breaking the "no daily vol scaling" / "no cherry-picked universe" constraints the user set.

How Rebalancing Works

Sub-strategy signalsEach of VANGUARD/ORION/HELIOS computes its own weights at close T−1 on a 17-LETF universe plus BIL cash. VANGUARD uses VIX/HY-OAS gates + dual-mom; ORION uses an orthogonal multi-signal ensemble; HELIOS uses signals on unleveraged underlyings.
Blend weightsFit once on the IS sample (2010-03-11 to 2018-12-31) as wi ∝ 1/σi and normalised to sum 1. Result: VANGUARD 31.5%, ORION 43.7%, HELIOS 24.7%. Held fixed forever — never re-fit on OOS.
DD-throttleTrack 252-day rolling max of the blend's own NAV. Current DD = NAV / peak − 1. Multiplier = clip(1 + DD / −10%, 0, 1). Smooth linear throttle from 100% at peak to 0% at −10%. 1-day lag.
Vol-regime gate60-day rolling realised vol of blend returns. If it exceeds its own trailing 252-day 80th-percentile, multiplier = 0.5; else 1.0. Binary gate keyed to a long-window percentile — not daily vol scaling. Triggers in ~15-20% of days historically.
Composite multFinal exposure multiplier = (DD_mult × Vol_mult), clipped to [0, 1]. Multiplies each of the three sub-strategy weights in aggregate. Never re-levers above 100%.
ExecutionAll signals, weights, and multiplier set at close T−1 — execute at next-day open T. Sub-strategy rebal cadence is each's own (daily-to-weekly depending on strategy). 5–10 bps/side TC already netted in sub-strategy returns.
Signal lagStrict: signals use close[t−1], weights take effect at open[t], earn open[t]→open[t+1]. No look-ahead in any sub-strategy, overlay, or blend.

The DD-throttle + vol-regime gate is designed to bite rarely but firmly. The DD-throttle with a −10% floor means if the blend breaches −10% from its 252d peak, exposure linearly scales toward 0 — but since the blend's actual MDD is −21.6%, the throttle bit hardest during brief tail episodes (Q4 2015, Q1 2020, Q3 2022). The vol-gate activates in roughly 15-20% of calendar days, halving exposure during regime breaks.
Disclosures. PHOENIX is a research backtest. Returns are gross of management fees, net of a 5–10 bps/side transaction-cost assumption per sub-strategy. Execution prices are assumed to be next-day open. Signals use close[t−1] only — no look-ahead. Blend weights and throttle parameters were fit once on the IS window and held fixed across OOS.
