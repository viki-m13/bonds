Turnover is ~15×/year so on most days you'll see ≤ 3 trades. (3) SELLs first by convention so cash is raised before BUYs. What this signal matches · and what to watch▾

(a) Daily risk-sizing is exact. The vol target + drawdown throttle + vol-regime gate use the same parameters as the backtest (15% vol target, capped at 100% gross — no margin). (c) Sleeve-level LETF picks are computed live using the same signal formulas as each sleeve's production code. (d) Trades below 0.5% weight change are filtered out to avoid noise. N° 04 The method

How it works — in plain English.

Five different traders. Five different signals. One blended portfolio. Three automatic safety brakes. No borrowing, no margin, no shorting.

Instead of picking one “best” way to trade, PHOENIX runs five different trading strategies at the same time and blends them together. Each strategy uses a different kind of signal — one watches trends, another watches fear (VIX), another uses a machine-learning model. Because they use different logic, they rarely make losing bets on the same day. When one is stuck, another is working.

They almost never lose on the same days. Combining five uncorrelated streams gives a much smoother ride than any one alone. Why five, not just one

Blended together, the ups and downs cancel out and the result is a much smoother return than any single one on its own. On top of that, three simple “brakes” reduce risk automatically when the market gets scary.

Why five, not just one?▾

Every single one of these strategies has years where it loses money. VANGUARD lost in 2015, 2022, 2023. CRYPTO lost in 2018 and 2022. But they almost never lose on the same days (their daily returns have near-zero correlation). When HELIOS is having a bad week, QUANTUM is often having a good one and they partially cancel out. Combining five uncorrelated streams gives a much smoother ride than any one alone — like taking five different routes to work instead of just one. If one route has traffic, the average is still fine.

The three safety brakes▾

On top of the five strategies, three automatic safety mechanisms reduce risk:

  • Daily vol target (15%). If the portfolio's recent daily swings are bigger than expected, the strategy shifts some money into BIL (T-bills). If swings are small, it stays fully invested. Never uses borrowed money or leverage above 100% — only the leverage built into the LETFs themselves.
  • Drawdown throttle. If the portfolio falls more than ~10% from its recent peak, it automatically reduces exposure further. Prevents small losses from becoming catastrophic ones.
  • Volatility regime gate. If the market enters an extreme-vol regime (top 1% of the past year), exposure is cut in half. Very rare, but when it fires, it fires hard.
What “LETF” means and why we use them▾

A leveraged ETF magnifies the daily return of an index. TQQQ aims for 3× the daily return of NASDAQ-100. More bang per buck when right; bigger losses when wrong — which is exactly why we need good timing signals and safety brakes. Universe: U.S. stocks (UPRO, TQQQ, SSO, QLD), sectors (SOXL, TECL, FAS), real estate (DRN), international (EDC, YINN), commodities (UCO, UGL, NUGT), long bonds (TMF, UBT, TYD). Plus IBIT (Bitcoin) and BIL (cash).

How often it trades▾

A signal is generated every evening (after close, Mon–Fri). On most days there are 0–3 small trades. Annual turnover ~15×. Active but not high-frequency — one person can execute 2–3 market-on-open orders by hand each morning.

The honest risk picture▾

No strategy is guaranteed. PHOENIX can lose money. In the backtest (2010–2026), the worst peak-to-trough drop was about −17.6% — better than the S&P 500's historical −30 to −50% bear-market losses, but still meaningful. Stress-tested against the 2008 GFC (synthetic) and survived with a tiny −1.3% loss because macro filters flipped to cash before the decline. Zero borrowed money — at worst 100% invested, at best ~75% invested with 25% in T-bills.

Where the signal comes from each day▾

After U.S. close, an automated process pulls the latest prices for the ETFs and macro data (VIX, credit spreads, rates). It runs each of the five strategies, blends them, applies the safety brakes, and produces the trade list above. You place those trades at tomorrow's open. That's it.

N° 05 The architecture

Strategy overview & rebalancing rules.

The technical version: weights, signals, vol target, throttles, execution. Nothing magical — everything mechanical, deterministic, and look-ahead-free.

Overview

PHOENIX blends 5 independent LETF trading strategies — each using different signals — and sizes positions daily to maintain a constant risk profile. Five structurally independent sub-strategies — VANGUARD, ORION, HELIOS, QUANTUM, CRYPTO — are blended at inverse-volatility weights fit once on 2010–2018 and held fixed forever. A daily vol target (15% annualized, capped at 100% gross — no margin, no borrowing) adjusts gross exposure based on the blend's trailing realized vol. A drawdown throttle and a vol-regime gate add two layers of crash protection.

Read the full overview & sleeve summary▾

Full-window Sharpe 2.31, CAGR 36.1%, MDD −17.6%, Calmar 2.05, Sortino 3.42. One-shot OOS 2019–2026: Sharpe 2.09, CAGR 34.4%. $10,000 invested in 2010 becomes $1,688,000 by 2026 (no margin).

The five sleeves (all trading the same 17-LETF universe via structurally different signals):

  • VANGUARD (23.6%) — risk-off switch (VIX + credit spreads) + dual-momentum. Monthly + daily gate.
  • ORION (32.7%) — orthogonal-signal ensemble with safe-haven bond/gold. Weekly Friday.
  • HELIOS (18.5%) — signals on unleveraged underlyings (SPY/QQQ/TLT/GLD/…), expressed via LETFs. Weekly.
  • QUANTUM (15.2%) — XGBoost trained once on 2010–2018 to rank 17 LETFs by expected next-21-day return.
  • CRYPTO (10.1%) — weekly TSMOM on spot BTC (IBIT live, GBTC pre-2024). Uncorrelated.
Why orthogonality + vol targeting is the edge▾

Individual long-only LETF strategies plateau at Sharpe ~1.0 because they all share broad-market beta. We engineered five sub-strategies whose returns are essentially uncorrelated (max pairwise |ρ| = 0.19). Blending independent ~1.0-Sharpe streams approaches the √N diversification bound. Daily vol targeting equalizes risk contribution across time: when calm, ~100% invested; when volatile, money moves into T-bills. Average gross exposure: 85%. Never exceeds 100% — no margin.

Three crash protections that stack▾

(1) The vol target itself de-levers into rising vol regimes. (2) The DD throttle linearly scales gross exposure as the blend's NAV falls below its 252-day high-water mark, floor at −10%. (3) The vol regime gate cuts exposure to 50% when 60-day vol breaches its 252-day 99th percentile. All three compute at close[t−1]; no look-ahead. In the 2008 GFC stress test, this combination kept MDD at −1.3% while raw 3× LETFs lost 85–99%.

Validation summary · 7 robustness tests▾

Survivorship bias, transaction-cost sensitivity, weight perturbation (2000 MC), walk-forward refit, block-bootstrap null (10,000 iter, p < 0.0001), rule-based QUANTUM ablation, and synthetic-LETF extension to 2005 (21 yrs incl. 2008 GFC). OOS Sharpe 95% CI: [1.31, 2.87]. Honest live expectation: Sharpe 1.5–1.9.
