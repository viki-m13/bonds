The rules

1 · Regime

Risk-on: SPY ≥ its 200-day MA and >40% of S&P members above their own 200-day MA. Otherwise risk-off.

2 · Risk-on buy

Top-2 by rank(multi-horizon momentum) + 5 × rank(dollar volume). Momentum = sum of return ranks over 3/6/9/12 months, each skipping the last month.

3 · Risk-off buy

Keep buying. Among names with long-term uptrend intact (above 400-day MA or +24-month return) trading 30–60% below all-time high: top-2 by rank(discount) + rank(dollar volume).

4 · Execution & holding

Signal at close, buy at next open, half the contribution per name. Never sell. No stops, no panic exits, no recovery triggers — each tested and rejected.

Validation

  • Point-in-time universe: S&P 500 membership 1996–2026; no name selectable outside its membership window; delisting-aware accounting.
  • Leakage audit: panel hard-truncated at random dates, signals recomputed — zero difference. Execution strictly next-open.
  • Robustness: win-rate 93–95% across all 10 schedule phases; unchanged at 5→40 bps costs; k=1/2/3 → 95/93/91%; monthly cadence equivalent; broad parameter plateaus.
  • Split-sample: 2006–2014 starts 90% win vs QQQ; 2015–2023 starts 99%.
  • Transfer: NASDAQ-100 PIT universe (2015+) — beats QQQ-DCA at all 15 start dates.
  • Survivorship control: random picks from the same universe ≈ SPY and beat QQQ in only 8% of windows — the edge is selection, not the universe.
  • Honest negatives: LightGBM ranker (OOS IC≈0), Chronos re-ranking, vol-compression breakouts, volume signals, low-vol tilts, all sell rules — tested, failed, documented in dca/research/.
Caveats. Free price data lacks ~26% of historical constituents (mostly pre-2015 delistings) — mitigated by the random-pick control and the mega-cap tilt, but residual upward bias can't be fully excluded; apply a ~3% CAGR mental haircut. Pre-2004 (dot-com) behavior is untested. The 16 windows that still lose to QQQ (worst −10.6%) are 3–5-year windows starting 2010–2013, peak AAPL-concentration era. Past performance ≠ future results.
