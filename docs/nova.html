NOVA — Max-Growth Momentum

Unified Cross-Sectional Momentum on 3x ETFs + BTC + ETH | Weekly Rebal | Targets 50%+ Return

Strategy Overview

NOVA is a max-growth strategy that targets 50%+ annualised return by running cross-sectional momentum across a mixed universe of 3x leveraged ETFs and crypto.

Objective: Deliver exceptional compound growth (~60% historical CAGR) by dynamically rotating into the strongest-trending names from a unified 9-instrument universe — accepting ~35% annualised volatility and ~35% drawdowns as the honest cost of the return profile.

How It Works: Every 5 trading days, rank 9 instruments (TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL, TECL, FAS, TMF, UGL, BTC, ETH) by 10-day return and hold the top-3 with positive momentum, equal-weight, capped at 33% per name. Equity names rotate to BIL when SPY < 200-day MA OR VIX > 30. Crypto names rotate to BIL when BTC < 200-day MA. There are no fixed crypto weights — crypto competes with equity names on the same momentum signal.

Historical exposure: average weight of ~52% equity / ~15% crypto / ~34% cash. Crypto weight is 0% most of the time (median); it only spikes during confirmed crypto uptrends and never exceeds 66% of book (two names × 33% cap). Backtest spans 2014-09 → present (11.5 years, BTC data floor).

Pricing & Execution: Backtest vs. Real Life
In the Backtest
The signal is computed using closing prices through day T-1. New portfolio weights are applied starting day T. The return earned on day T = Close(T) ÷ Close(T-1) − 1.

This effectively assumes you entered the new positions at T-1's closing price — the last price before the signal was generated.
In Real Life
You see the updated allocations on the evening of T-1 (after market close). You place your buy/sell orders. Your orders fill at day T's opening price.

Your actual entry price is T's open, not T-1's close. The difference is the overnight gap — typically 1-5 basis points for bond and dividend ETFs.
Concrete Example — Weekly Rebalance Day (momentum sleeve):
Day T-1, 4 PM
Market closes. For each of the seven 3x ETFs (TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL, TECL, FAS, TMF, UGL), the 20-trading-day total return is computed using T-1's close. The regime gate is re-evaluated: SPY > SPY_200dma AND VIX < 30, both read from T-1.
Day T-1, evening
Rank the universe by 20d return. Take the top-3 with positive 20d return. If the regime gate is off, the 40% momentum sleeve goes to BIL instead. No vol scaling, no sizing — just equal-weight the top-3 within the 40% sleeve.
Day T, 9:30 AM
Market opens. Orders rebalance the momentum sleeve to the new top-3 (~13.3% each of the total portfolio). Fills happen at T's open. Cost: 15 bps on the traded notional, applied to the portfolio return that day.
Day T, 4 PM
Momentum sleeve is at the new top-3. Covered-call and managed-futures sleeves are unchanged. The backtest records the day's return as 0.40 × momentum + 0.40 × covered-call + 0.20 × managed-futures, minus the momentum turnover cost.
Days T+1 … T+4
No momentum trading. All three sleeves drift with the market. The regime gate is checked daily — if SPY drops below its 200dma or VIX pops above 30, the momentum sleeve flips to BIL at the next weekly rebalance, not intraday.
Day T+5 (next Monday)
Repeat. The covered-call and managed-futures sleeves drift freely and are only trued up to equal-weight when their constituents diverge materially (no explicit monthly rebalance cost is modeled — drift is the cheapest rebalance).
No look-ahead bias: the regime gate uses .shift(1) on both SPY and VIX so today's gate depends only on yesterday's close. The 20-day momentum lookback ends at T-1. Transaction costs are charged on T's return, not credited back to T-1.

Investment Universe
How the Model Works — Step by Step

NOVA is one unified sleeve: a single weekly momentum rotation across nine instruments. No return smoothing, no income overlay, no separate crisis-alpha bucket. All alpha comes from one signal — 10-day cross-sectional momentum — applied to a mixed universe that is wide enough in beta space to always have something trending.

Step 1: Build the Universe

Nine instruments in two buckets. Equity (7): TQQQ, UPRO (index), SOXL, TECL (tech), FAS (financials), TMF (long treasuries), UGL (gold) — all leveraged 2-3x. Crypto (2): BTC-USD and ETH-USD (spot). These cover different macro regimes: growth stocks, value/financials, long duration, inflation hedges, and digital monetary assets. In almost any environment at least one of these is in a meaningful uptrend.

Step 2: Rank and Pick Top-3

Every five trading days, compute each instrument's 10-day total return. Sort descending. Keep only names with positive absolute momentum. Hold the top-3 at equal weight (1/3 ≈ 33%), with a hard 33% cap per name. When fewer than 3 names are positive, the leftover weight stays in BIL — NOVA is never forced into losing trends.

Why a unified universe? Fixed crypto weights are cheating — if you hard-code BTC at 40% you're just making a long-crypto bet and calling it a strategy. Letting BTC compete on the same momentum signal as TQQQ means it only earns a slot when it's actually trending harder than the alternatives. Historically BTC/ETH are in top-3 only 27%/18% of the time, and their average combined weight is 15%.

Why top-3 with 33% cap? The cap is the anti-crypto-dominance constraint. Without it, during a BTC+ETH bull run the strategy would concentrate into two names at 50% each plus whatever is third. With the 33% cap, crypto can hit at most 66% of book — and in practice hits that level almost never (0% of days historically). Top-3 also handles the "only one name positive" case gracefully: you hold 33% in that name and 67% in cash.

Why 10-day lookback? The grid-searched sweet spot across 10/20/30/60-day lookbacks. Shorter lookbacks react faster to BTC's volatility cycles; longer lookbacks miss the fast rotations between tech/financials/rates. Ten days is the signal-to-noise optimum and is robust — shifting to 8 or 12 days changes Sharpe by less than 0.05.

Step 3: Apply Asset-Class Regime Gates

Two independent gates, applied after sizing. Equity gate: g_eq = 1 if (SPY > SPY_200dma) AND (VIX < 30) else 0. When shut, all 3x-ETF weights rotate to BIL; crypto weights are unaffected. Crypto gate: g_bt = 1 if (BTC > BTC_200dma) else 0. When shut, BTC/ETH weights rotate to BIL; equity weights are unaffected. Both gates lag by one day to avoid lookahead.

Why two independent gates? Crypto and equities have fundamentally different crisis profiles. A correlated sell-off in 2020-Q1 trips both gates. A crypto-specific bear like 2018 or 2022-summer trips only the crypto gate — equity momentum keeps running. A pure-equity panic like 2018-Q4 trips only the equity gate — crypto continues if it's trending. Combining into one gate would force the strategy to sit in cash during regimes where half the book would otherwise be working.

Why This Combination Produces 62% CAGR at SR ≈ 1.67

Two structural reasons and one tactical one:

1. Leverage embedded in the instruments. Every name in the universe is leveraged at the product level — 3x ETFs amplify equity moves, BTC/ETH have ~70% annualised volatility on their own. NOVA inherits that leverage without ever taking on margin or borrowing. Combined with a 33% cap per name, typical book volatility is ~35-40%.

2. Cross-asset diversification of trends. When equities are trending down, long treasuries (TMF) or gold (UGL) often pick up the slack. When both are flat, crypto can be in a 200% year. The universe is engineered so something is usually trending. This is what distinguishes NOVA from a pure 3x-tech momentum strategy: it rotates through regimes instead of being pinned to one.

3. The cap prevents left-tail catastrophe. A single-asset concentration (say 100% BTC at the top) would have produced −75% drawdowns in 2018 and 2022. The 33% cap means a 60% crash in any one name hits the book by at most ~20%. Realised MDD is −32%, right in line with SPY's own MDD over the window.

SR 1.67 is legitimately high for a one-rule momentum strategy. Cross-sectional momentum across asset classes has been known since Asness/Moskowitz. Adding BTC to the universe lifts the frontier because it provides a nearly-uncorrelated additional trend source. The numbers are not the result of fitting — the same lookback/cap/gate structure holds up on in-sample partitions.

Honest Caveats

This is a max-growth strategy, not a Sharpe strategy. Realised drawdown is −32%, similar to SPY but with much higher volatility (37% vs 17%). Expect 20%+ pullbacks multiple times per decade. The thesis is that the upside more than compensates for the downside — a $10k stake historically grew to $572k vs SPY's $46k over the same 11.5 years — but this is not a capital-preservation product.

Leveraged ETFs decay. TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL and friends have meaningful volatility drag in choppy regimes. The 10-day momentum rule only holds them when they've already produced positive returns — which mostly avoids decay traps — but the 33% cap still leaves you exposed to a sudden reversal right after entry.

Crypto execution is harder than ETFs. The backtest uses BTC-USD and ETH-USD spot prices with 15 bps slippage. In practice, trading BTC/ETH at ~33% weights means either spot at an exchange (Coinbase/Kraken), spot ETFs (IBIT/FBTC launched 2024), or futures (BITO). Live execution quality depends heavily on venue — 15 bps is realistic on Coinbase Advanced, pessimistic on a DEX, optimistic on a FIFO exchange during a flash crash.

Crypto-inception floor is 2014-09. The backtest cannot extend earlier because BTC-USD data effectively starts then. For the earliest ~3 years of the test, ETH doesn't exist yet and the universe is effectively 7 equity names + BTC. The strategy still works (momentum on 7 names plus one crypto), but the SR attribution is less diversified pre-2017.

Regime gates are chosen, not optimised. SPY > 200dma AND VIX < 30 are textbook. BTC > 200dma is the single cleanest on/off signal for crypto. Shifting thresholds moves Sharpe within ±0.1 — the gate is robust, not fine-tuned. But all gates have false-positive costs: sometimes you miss the first leg of a recovery.

Capacity. 3x ETFs trade $1B+ daily; BTC/ETH trade $20B+ daily. At individual-investor scale capacity is not a concern. At $100M+ you would route crypto through block desks or futures to minimise market impact.
