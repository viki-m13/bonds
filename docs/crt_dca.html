The edge is front-loaded — era by era

Split the 23 years into four non-overlapping eras and DCA within each. The 2003–2009 crash-recovery era carries almost the entire edge; in every later era the lead over QQQ-DCA shrinks to single-to-low-double digits, and the most recent era is the slimmest.

Read this honestly. The strategy genuinely beats S&P-DCA and QQQ-DCA in all four eras on this stream — but the magnitude is overwhelmingly a one-off: ~+120 pp/yr in the 2003–2009 GFC recovery, fading to a single-digit lead over QQQ in 2021–2026. This is mechanically a crash-recovery / high-beta amplifier, which is also why the account fell ~−56% peak-to-trough. The repeatable, steady-state edge over a growth benchmark is a fraction of the 55%/yr headline.

What a clean retrain shows

The 55%/yr stream above is the production pipeline's own output. When a faithful walk-forward retrain of a comparable recipe is forced to train and pick on strictly point-in-time membership — no cached-universe survivorship — the DCA edge over a growth benchmark disappears. (Source: the CRT repo's own research/validation/REPORT.md.)
The two layers, side by side. The published stream beats QQQ-DCA on every horizon; an independent walk-forward retrain on clean PIT data loses to QQQ-DCA by ~9–11 pp/yr and barely matches (or trails) S&P-DCA. The truth is in between and depends on how much of the production stream's 2003–2009 magnitude survives out of sample. Treat the headline as "real but front-loaded and beta-driven," not as a durable 55%/yr selection edge.

How this differs from the live site

Three honest additions to the dailystockguide.com presentation, all using the site's own data.

1 · Add the QQQ benchmark

The site benchmarks only against S&P-DCA. A picker that tilts to high-beta growth should beat the S&P — the honest test is whether it beats QQQ-DCA. Here it does on the production stream, but the lead is far smaller and vanishes on a clean retrain.

2 · Lead the era table, not the headline

The "100% of 10y windows" stat is true but lets one era do the work. Showing the four-era breakdown up front makes the front-loading impossible to miss: ~+120 pp/yr then fading to single digits.

3 · Show the clean-retrain number

The CRT repo already contains an honest walk-forward PIT validation (~11% CAGR, loses to QQQ). Publishing it next to the 55% headline is the single most credibility-building change.

Keep what's already good

The site's methodology hygiene is genuinely strong — real walk-forward + 7-month embargo + PIT membership + measured survivorship correction + frank drawdown/front-loading disclosures. This page builds on that, it doesn't contradict it.
