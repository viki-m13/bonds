The rules (as replicated)

1 · Leadership score

Rank by average(3-month, 6-month return) × fraction of rising weeks over 6 months. "Strong AND steady beats strong alone."

2 · MAX screen

Exclude any name whose biggest 1-day jump in the past month is in the universe's top 5% — "names that just went vertical tend to give it back."

3 · Hold & rotate

Hold the top 3, equal weight. Keep a position while it stays in the top 8; sell once it drops below rank 8. Buy at next open.

4 · Cash switch

If SPY closes below its 210-day average, sell everything to cash; re-enter at the first rebalance after SPY reclaims it.

Validation

  • Causality: leadership score and sell rule both pass the truncation leakage audit (panel hard-truncated at random dates, recomputed — zero difference). Execution strictly next-open.
  • Same harness as SUMMIT: identical point-in-time S&P 500 universe (no survivorship), delisting-aware engine, 244-window grid, regime windows, random-pick control.
  • Cost honesty: ROTATOR turns over ~26×/yr, so it is cost-sensitive (full multiple 32.8× at 5 bps → 30.0× at 10 bps); shown at its stated 10 bps/side.
  • Concentration of edge: removing the 2023-2026 window collapses most of ROTATOR's outperformance — its rolling 3/5/10-year win-rates vs QQQ are 51/50/52%.
Caveats. Our replication is faithful to the published v2.0 spec but rendered in the DCA harness (regime checked at each biweekly date, not intraday) on the S&P 500 PIT universe (the original adds the Nasdaq-100). Free price data lacks ~26% of historical constituents (mostly pre-2015 delistings); the mega-/large-cap leadership tilt sits in the best-covered names, but residual survivorship bias can't be fully excluded. ROTATOR will draw down 35-50% and can trail for years when leadership is broad. Concentration is real (today: one storage/memory theme). ~26 short-term-gain sales/yr — hold in a tax-advantaged account. Past performance ≠ future results. Not investment advice.
