APEX — Multi-Sleeve Leveraged-ETF Ensemble

6-sleeve blend: 50% XGBoost ML alpha engine + 50% rule-based | Long-only, no portfolio margin | 1999-2026, 27 yrs | IS 2005-2018 / OOS 2019+ |

Strategy Overview

Objective: Harvest multiple uncorrelated alpha sources using leveraged ETFs, following the PHOENIX playbook with additional LETF-focused sleeves and more aggressive crisis hedging. Long-only. No portfolio margin — all leverage comes from the 2× / 3× ETFs themselves.

Architecture: Five sleeves, equal-weighted (20% each). Each sleeve emits daily target weights that sum to ≤ 1. Sleeves are individually vol-scaled (18% annualised, 28% for ML5) before blending. Each sleeve targets a distinct signal family to minimise mutual correlation — following the PHOENIX “uncorrelated-sleeves” playbook:

  • V1 MOM_LEV (20%) — Phoenix-Vanguard clone. Monthly 189-day momentum on the 4-LETF defensive basket {QLD, UGL, TMF, TYD}. Top-2 by momentum, inverse-60d-vol weighted, 200-day MA filter, VIX < 30 gate.
  • V2 RISK_SAFE (20%) — Phoenix-Orion clone. 50/50 split of RISK (top-3 of 7 equity LETFs by 252d mom, VIX-gated) + SAFE (top-2 of 4 bond/gold LETFs by 0.7·mom + 0.3·low-vol composite). Weekly rebalance.
  • V3 SECTOR (20%) — Top-1 of {TECL, FAS, SOXL, ERX, DRN} by 63-day momentum, weekly. Market-filtered by SPY > 200d MA and VIX < 30.
  • V4 ML5 (20%) — XGBoost regressor predicting 5-day forward log return. Features: 5/21/63/126/252d momentum, 21/63d vol & Sharpe, relative-to-SPY returns, distance-to-200dMA, VIX, T10Y2Y curve, HYG/LQD credit proxy, cross-sectional ranks. Multi-horizon CV selects horizon N from {5, 10, 21, 42}d by rank-IC. Top-3 picks, weekly rebalance. Trained strictly on IS (2005-2018).
  • V6 SHORT_MR (20%) — Short-term mean reversion: buy equity LETFs when RSI(2) < 5 AND asset > 200d MA AND SPY > 200d MA. Hold 3 days.

Portfolio overlays (Phoenix-style, lagged 1 day):

  1. Vol-regime gate: halve portfolio exposure when SPY 60d realised vol > 99th percentile of trailing 504 days.
  2. Drawdown throttle: linear 0→1 scaling from 0% to -12% NAV drawdown vs 252d HWM.
  3. Vol target: 22% annualised. Bidirectional (scale up when realised vol is below target, down when above) — but gross-capped at 100% of capital. No portfolio margin.
⚠ Honest reporting: The stated design objective was Sharpe 3+ OOS / CAGR 50%+. APEX does not meet this target. Achieved: OOS Sharpe 0.97, CAGR 20.5%. Full-sample (1999-2026) Sharpe 0.91, CAGR 16.6%. APEX does beat PHOENIX-Extended (the apples-to-apples 2005+ synthetic comparison) on full-period Sharpe (1.04 vs 0.52), but loses badly to real PHOENIX on its native 2010+ window (1.03 vs 2.33) — and most painfully, loses −35.9% in 2022 where PHOENIX was flat. See the head-to-head table below. Sharpe 3+ OOS long-only, no-margin, on a broad LETF universe with pre-2008 stress testing appears to be above the achievable frontier — PHOENIX at 2.14 OOS is the repo's frontier and even it fell short of 3. APEX was built over eight iterations (v1-v8) experimenting with trend, cross-sectional momentum, risk parity, credit regime, vol regime, ML, mean-reversion, calendar, and yield-curve signals. The research code for every experiment is preserved in apex/ for audit.

Takeaway. PHOENIX dominates in post-GFC regimes (2010+) because of two features APEX lacks: (i) the QUANTUM XGBoost sleeve trained on a clean post-GFC dataset, and (ii) a tight composite macro gate (VIX + HY-OAS + yield-curve + SPY 200-MA) that exits aggressively ahead of crashes — the reason PHOENIX's 2008 MDD is only −1.3%. APEX wins whenever the test window is the 2005+ synthetic set Phoenix-Extended uses, because APEX was engineered with that deeper stress test in view. Neither strategy hits the 3+ Sharpe / 50%+ CAGR objective on an OOS 2019+ basis; PHOENIX at 2.14 OOS is closest, and that appears to be near the achievable frontier for long-only, no-margin LETF strategies.

APEX-Phoenix return correlation (2010-2026): 0.44 — the two strategies share less than half of their daily variance, so a 50/50 blend would further diversify.
