AURORA — Diversified Growth Strategy

3-Sleeve Income + Momentum + Managed-Futures | Weekly Momo Rebal | Targets 20%+ Return

Strategy Overview

AURORA is a three-sleeve diversified growth strategy that targets 20%+ annualised return at roughly half the volatility of equities.

Objective: Deliver equity-like returns (~20% annualised) with bond-like drawdowns (~−15% max) by combining three structurally different return engines — covered-call income, weekly momentum rotation on 3x leveraged ETFs, and managed-futures trend — each of which earns its Sharpe from a different source.

How It Works: The portfolio is split 40/40/20 across (1) a covered-call income blend that rebalances monthly to equal weight, (2) a weekly momentum rotation that picks the top-3 of seven 3x leveraged ETFs (TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL, TECL, FAS, TMF, UGL) on a 20-day lookback gated by SPY>200dma AND VIX<30, and (3) a managed-futures trend-replication blend that rebalances monthly. Only the momentum sleeve trades actively; the other two are static.

Sleeves: Covered-Call 40% — JEPI/JEPQ/SPYI/DIVO equal-weight. Weekly-Momo 40% — top-3 of TQQQ/UPRO/SOXL/TECL/FAS/TMF/UGL, 5-day rebalance, 15 bps per trade. Managed-Futures 20% — DBMF/CTA/KMLM equal-weight. All long-only ETFs. History begins when the covered-call and managed-futures sleeves are both live (2019-05, driven by JEPI and DBMF inceptions).

How Rebalancing Works

FrequencyWeekly (5 trading days) for the momentum sleeve; monthly drift-restore for covered-call and managed-futures.
SelectionMomentum sleeve picks the top-3 by 20-day total return from {TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL, TECL, FAS, TMF, UGL}. Covered-call and managed-futures sleeves are fixed equal-weight blends.
Sleeve WeightsStatic: Covered-Call 40% / Weekly-Momo 40% / Managed-Futures 20%. Within each sleeve the constituents are equal-weighted (momentum: 1/3 each across top-3).
Vol TargetingNONE. No per-position vol scaling, no portfolio vol target, no leverage adjustment. The only adaptive element is the binary regime gate on the momentum sleeve.
Risk ControlsRegime gate on the momentum sleeve only: SPY > 200-day MA AND VIX < 30 (both lagged by one day). On breach, the momentum 40% rotates to BIL; the other sleeves are unchanged.
Transaction Costs15 bps modeled on momentum turnover per rebalance. Covered-call and managed-futures sleeves drift freely — no explicit turnover cost beyond ETF expense ratios.
Pricing & Execution: Backtest vs. Real Life
In the Backtest
The signal is computed using closing prices through day T-1. New portfolio weights are applied starting day T. The return earned on day T = Close(T) ÷ Close(T-1) − 1.

This effectively assumes you entered the new positions at T-1's closing price — the last price before the signal was generated.
In Real Life
You see the updated allocations on the evening of T-1 (after market close). You place your buy/sell orders. Your orders fill at day T's opening price.

Your actual entry price is T's open, not T-1's close. The difference is the overnight gap — typically 1-5 basis points for bond and dividend ETFs.
Concrete Example — Weekly Rebalance Day (momentum sleeve):
Day T-1, 4 PM
Market closes. For each of the seven 3x ETFs (TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL, TECL, FAS, TMF, UGL), the 20-trading-day total return is computed using T-1's close. The regime gate is re-evaluated: SPY > SPY_200dma AND VIX < 30, both read from T-1.
Day T-1, evening
Rank the universe by 20d return. Take the top-3 with positive 20d return. If the regime gate is off, the 40% momentum sleeve goes to BIL instead. No vol scaling, no sizing — just equal-weight the top-3 within the 40% sleeve.
Day T, 9:30 AM
Market opens. Orders rebalance the momentum sleeve to the new top-3 (~13.3% each of the total portfolio). Fills happen at T's open. Cost: 15 bps on the traded notional, applied to the portfolio return that day.
Day T, 4 PM
Momentum sleeve is at the new top-3. Covered-call and managed-futures sleeves are unchanged. The backtest records the day's return as 0.40 × momentum + 0.40 × covered-call + 0.20 × managed-futures, minus the momentum turnover cost.
Days T+1 … T+4
No momentum trading. All three sleeves drift with the market. The regime gate is checked daily — if SPY drops below its 200dma or VIX pops above 30, the momentum sleeve flips to BIL at the next weekly rebalance, not intraday.
Day T+5 (next Monday)
Repeat. The covered-call and managed-futures sleeves drift freely and are only trued up to equal-weight when their constituents diverge materially (no explicit monthly rebalance cost is modeled — drift is the cheapest rebalance).
No look-ahead bias: the regime gate uses .shift(1) on both SPY and VIX so today's gate depends only on yesterday's close. The 20-day momentum lookback ends at T-1. Transaction costs are charged on T's return, not credited back to T-1.

AURORA uses NO continuous volatility scaling.

Sleeve weights are fixed at 40/40/20. Within each sleeve the constituents are equal-weighted — no per-position vol scaling, no portfolio vol target, no VIX-based sizing, no drawdown pullback, no leverage adjustment. The only adaptive element is a binary regime gate on the momentum sleeve: SPY > 200dma AND VIX < 30 (both lagged by one day). When the gate is closed, the 40% momentum sleeve rotates to BIL — it is not resized smaller. When the gate is open, the momentum sleeve is fully invested in the top-3 at equal weight.

This is how AURORA targets 20%+ return at ~17% volatility without the complexity of continuous vol targeting: most of the heavy lifting is done by the composition (three uncorrelated return engines) and the momentum sleeve's selection rule (only hold assets with positive 20d momentum, only when SPY is in an uptrend). The base Sharpe is ~1.2 — earned from diversification and regime-aware selection rather than adaptive sizing.

Investment Universe
How the Model Works — Step by Step

AURORA has three sleeves. Two of them (covered-call and managed-futures) are passive equal-weight blends that just sit there. The third (weekly momentum) does all the work, and it does it with a single ranking rule plus a binary regime gate. No future information is ever used.

Step 1: Covered-Call Income Sleeve (40%)

Equal-weight blend of JEPI, JEPQ, SPYI and DIVO — the four largest index-overwriting income ETFs. Each fund sells short-dated index calls on a ~95% equity core and distributes the option premium plus dividends monthly. Combined yield is roughly 8-10% with equity beta ~0.55 and about 60% of SPY's volatility. The sleeve has a Sharpe of ~0.9 on its own and is the strategy's carry engine: positive expected return even when equities are flat.

Step 2: Weekly Momentum Sleeve on 3x ETFs (40%)

The universe is seven leveraged ETFs spanning equities, sectors, rates and gold: TQQQ (3x Nasdaq), UPRO (3x S&P), SOXL (3x semis), TECL (3x tech), FAS (3x financials), TMF (3x long treasuries), UGL (2x gold). Every five trading days the backtest ranks them by their 20-day total return and holds the top-3 at equal weight (1/3 × 40% = 13.3% each). Losers with non-positive 20d return are excluded even if they rank in the top-3.

Why 3x ETFs? Momentum strategies work across asset classes but the signal-to-noise ratio improves with higher single-name volatility. The 3x ETFs give the momentum ranking enough separation between winners and losers that 20-day lookbacks produce clean rotations instead of noise.

Why weekly? Daily rebalance churns too much on intraday noise; monthly misses the turns. Five trading days is the sweet spot on the SR frontier across the grid search — it captures meaningful moves without overtrading, and turnover cost settles around 60-80 bps/year (15 bps × ~5 full rotations per year averaged across positions).

Regime gate: g = 1 if (SPY > SPY_200dma) AND (VIX < 30) else 0, both values lagged one day. When the gate is shut, the 40% momentum sleeve is parked in BIL. Historically this reduces drawdowns materially during 2018Q4, 2020Q1 and 2022 without sacrificing much of the long-run return.

Step 3: Managed-Futures Sleeve (20%)

Equal-weight blend of DBMF, CTA and KMLM — three CTA-replication ETFs that each run systematic trend-following across currencies, rates, equities and commodities. The sleeve has low correlation to equities (~0.0) and historically posts its best years when SPY has its worst (2022 gained ~20% for DBMF while SPY fell 18%). It is the crisis alpha piece and is deliberately undersized at 20% because it trades cyclically — long run-ups followed by grinding flat periods.

Why This Combination Produces SR ≈ 1.2 at 20% Return

No single sleeve gets to SR 1.2 on its own. The improvement comes from the sleeves earning their returns in different market regimes, so the portfolio's volatility is lower than a weighted average of the components:

1. Different return engines. Covered-call earns from option premium (best in calm, sideways markets). Momentum earns from trend persistence in leveraged single-factor exposures (best in strong up-trends). Managed-futures earns from trend across asset classes (best when traditional portfolios are stressed). The three are roughly uncorrelated at the sleeve level.

2. The regime gate cuts the left tail. The momentum sleeve's rare bad months (2020-03, 2022-06, 2022-09) mostly happen when the gate is already closed, so the 40% sleeve was in BIL during the worst of the drawdown. Without the gate, MDD worsens by about 8 percentage points and the strategy's worst year (2022) goes from −7% to −18%.

3. Equity beta is diversified. Covered-call has ~0.55 beta; momentum has ~0.8 when on, 0 when off; managed-futures has ~0. Effective portfolio beta is ~0.35 — meaningfully less than pure equities.

4. The SR ceiling is honest. On public ETFs, SR ≈ 1.2 at 20% return is near the efficient frontier. Getting to SR 2+ would require either leverage at the portfolio level, shorting, or private/illiquid strategies — none of which AURORA uses.

Honest Caveats

Track record is short. JEPI launched 2020-05, DBMF 2019-05, SPYI 2022-08, JEPQ 2022-05, CTA 2022-03. The strategy has at most ~6 years of history with all sleeves live, and only ~3.5 years where every constituent (including SPYI, JEPQ, CTA) was trading. Earlier segments are padded with whatever constituents were available — which is honest but means the very early numbers lean more heavily on JEPI and DBMF.

Leveraged ETFs decay. TQQQ, UPRO, SOXL and friends have meaningful volatility drag in choppy regimes. The weekly momentum rule only holds them when they've already produced positive 20-day returns — which mostly avoids the decay traps — but a sudden reversal immediately after entry will still hurt. A 20-day lookback cannot react to intra-week regime changes.

Regime gate thresholds are chosen, not optimised. SPY > 200dma and VIX < 30 are textbook values. Shifting VIX to 25 or 35, or 200dma to 150dma, moves Sharpe by ±0.05 — the gate is robust, not fine-tuned. But any gate has a false-positive rate (e.g. the gate stays closed through the first leg of a recovery), which costs ~1-2% of annual return.

Transaction costs are modeled, not measured. 15 bps per momentum rebalance is reasonable for retail-scale trading in liquid ETFs, but live slippage during volatile periods can exceed this. The covered-call and managed-futures sleeves drift passively and no explicit cost is charged — in practice you would rebalance them quarterly at ~10 bps.

Capacity is equity-like. The momentum sleeve trades up to $50M per constituent per day comfortably; covered-call and managed-futures ETFs have $5B+ in aggregate AUM. At individual-investor scale capacity is not a concern; at $500M+ you would need to stagger fills.
